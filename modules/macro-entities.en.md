### 7.4.1 Ontological Foundation Transformation: From "What Constitutes" to "How Generated"

Traditional life view's cornerstone is **Ontology of Being**, with core questions: "**What is the life entity composed of?**" and "**What essential attributes make it life?**" Answers typically point to specific material foundations (carbon-based, water, cellular structure) and specific functional attributes (metabolism, reproduction, etc.). MVM's proposed new life view is built upon **Ontology of Manifestation** foundation. **Process Prior to Entity:** Life is no longer viewed as a "thing" but as an "event flow" or "process." Its "existence" is embodied in its continuous "generation" behavior. Certain insights from process philosophy (such as Whitehead's thought) find echo here.

**Relation Prior to Elements:** Life form's identity is not merely determined by its internal components, but more profoundly defined by **dynamic interactive relationships it establishes and maintains with its environment (other snapshots), with its potentiality foundation (interface atlas)**. Life is a **relational existence**.

**Information/Structure Prior to Matter:** Though manifestation process requires physical carriers (interfaces/execution environments), life's **core characteristics** are considered to more manifest in its capacity to **process information, invoke structures, generate meaning**, rather than its specific material form. This aligns with information philosophy and systems theory viewpoints.
